Get users for your AI tool from Google search

Disclaimer: This is some experience I have discovered in a short time as an engineer without any SEO basis, so there might be some fundamental errors. If so, you are more than welcome to email me to correct me.

Creating a Website

Even if what you are developing is an App or desktop software, I still suggest you create a website for your product, which has two advantages: 1) Showcasing your product is functionalities in a more rich way through diagrams, animations, videos, etc. 2) Enabling potential users to discover your product via Google search, which is a crucial source for user acquisition.

And once you have decided to make a website, there comes the question: how to increase the chances that your target users will click into your website when searching chat ai, which is where SEO (Search Engine Optimization) comes in.

PageRank Algorithm

The ultimate goal of SEO is to rank your product higher in Google search. Thus, it is necessary to understand some of Google’s ranking algorithms.

Although Google no longer uses PageRank as its only algorithm, it is still an important ranking factor: PageRank assumes that “more important pages are often cited by other pages more often”[1]. Similar to the impact factor of scientific journals, Google assigns a score (domain rating, DR) to each website: Google will rank the websites with higher DRs higher if they hit the same keyword, which makes sense.

So, how is the score calculated? A straightforward idea is to calculate the average score of the websites that link to your website, a behavior called backlink. In this way, the PageRank algorithm looks like a graph with weights: suppose your website is backlinked by three other websites, and the DRs of these websites are 100, 10, 1, respectively, then your website score would be $(100 * 1 + 10 * 1 + 1 * 1) * coefficient$. It is clear that the more backlinks are not necessarily the better, the quality of the websites providing you with backlinks is more important, and some algorithms only count the top 100 domains with the highest scores that link to your website, thus the method of continuously registering domains to hoard the number of backlinks is no longer effective.

Ahrefs website comments on domain rating as follows:

You should strive to get backlinks from high DR websites, as they have greater “weight” .[2]

Increasing Domain Rating

Therefore, a critical task for SEO is to increase the Domain Rating of your website. The next tasks are: 1) How to increase the number of backlinks. 2) How to get high-rating websites to backlink to me.

For the first task: you can buy domains and do nothing but backlink the new domain to your domain. Note that while it is a new domain, it can also be a website that has expired: these expired websites have accumulated a certain number of backlinks over the past many years, so their ratings are likely higher than the new domain (0), bringing more DR weight to your website.

I noticed an approach from a Twitter user:

  • Visit expireddomains.net to view expired domains.
  • Rank by their past DP (number of backlinks from different domains), check DR with ahrefs, check what these websites were for in the past with WebArchive, and find websites that are as related to your product category as possible (so their backlinks are usually in the same field).
  • Backlink these websites to your website, then wait for the DR to increase.

For the second task: directly approach high DR websites to add backlinks to your website. As this article is mainly for AI tools, it only discusses how to add backlinks to AI tools.

There is actually a two-sided market here: many new AI tools need exposure, while many AI tool list websites need new content, so they are willing to set up AI tool submission portals. Some websites are free, while some websites with a lot of users charge 10-20 dollars. You can decide whether it’s worth paying to submit based on your own judgment.

Choosing AI Tool List Websites

Just to share my approach: first, visit Similarweb, search for a tool list website, such as aitools.fyi, where you can see its traffic in the past few months, where visitors come from, gender and more.

Secondly, use Ahrefs Backlink Checker to check the DR of this website, to estimate the DR increase it will bring to your website once it includes you. An interesting thing is: you can actually enter your own tool website to check which websites are backlinking it, so you can have an intuitive understanding of “which websites have a high DR”. Note, The algorithm of DR scoring from Ahref is not the same with Google, but the trend is similar.

From the Similarweb screenshot above, you can see it provides a similar websites feature, so you can quickly find websites similar to aitools.fyi. In this way, repeat the above two steps, and in the end, you can make a judgment of “whether to pay to submit the tool to this website”.

Guidelines from Google Search Console [3]

Google only wants you to improve your web page’s load speed and compatibility. The faster and the better the compatibility, the higher the impression score. Here are some tips I have tried:

Preconnect

That is, add a line of preconnect code before accessing resources. For example, add a line before:

<link rel="stylesheet" href="https://cdnjs.cloudflare.com/ajax/libs/font-awesome/6.0.0-beta3/css/all.min.css">

It becomes:

<link rel="preconnect" href="https://cdnjs.cloudflare.com">
<link rel="stylesheet" href="https://cdnjs.cloudflare.com/ajax/libs/font-awesome/6.0.0-beta3/css/all.min.css">

This could speed up the resource acquisition speed, thereby speeding up the webpage loading.

Multiple resolutions for media resources

If you use images/videos on your webpage, try to add small/middle/large three resolutions:

<img srcset="small.jpg 600w, 
             medium.jpg 1200w, 
             large.jpg 1800w"
     sizes="(max-width: 600px) 600px,
            (max-width: 1200px) 1200px,
            1800px"
     src="large.jpg" 
     alt="Your image description">

Compatibility for mobile devices

Use responsive design frameworks (such as Tailwind CSS), and test on both desktop and mobile devices, make sure your webpage fits these two screen sizes, so that Google can score your webpage higher.

I used the above set of combination optimization methods on my website, and the average page load time decreased by 25% after one week.

The above is just the tip of the iceberg in SEO, but it is all the experience I can share at the moment, I hope it can help you increase the exposure of your AI tools.
