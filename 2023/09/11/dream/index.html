梦/Dream

在许多夜晚，我的梦里都存在两个我。聚会上，当别人喊我的名字，我抬头，总会看到另一个我同时抬头，代替我做出回应，我则坐在角落默不吭声，没人对此感到意外。

两个我，睁开眼就能看见另一个，万花筒一般的梦让我感到头晕和疲惫。梦里永远有一群人在聚会，我在角落喝着苦酒忍受身份折磨。在某个梦里，我在角落碰见了另一个倒霉鬼，他的处境和我一样。我问他，杀死替身有用吗？他说他试过，私下杀死的人会立刻重新长出来，他已经侵占了你的本体。除非在公开场合，让所有人看到你杀死了他，才能真正让他永不复生。说着，他望向自己的复制品。

房间满是欢声笑语的人。在昏暗的角落，他在桌底给我比手势，他想进行一场无差别的屠杀，把荒唐的记忆从所有人的梦中抹掉，我心领神会。很快，一个契机让灯灭了。漆黑中，我听到了杀戮和血。

灯亮了，死的是他。原来复制体之间共享思维。

我迅速打开窗户跳下去，逃走了。一路上我拼命跑，到了一片沼泽地。月光下，我回头看见了另一个我，我看不清他的脸，他应该也是。

我想起过往犯下的种种罪孽，有多少是他植入的潜意识？我恨他，真的。

“先别指责我，那次你不也玩得很开心吗？” 脑海里他说。
“闭嘴。” 我气急败坏。
“对了，那个让她气疯的电话，你应该不知道她正在开车吧？”
“是你让我打的。” 我蹲下来，狠狠地把地面砸出一个坑，土很软。

我不再理睬他，转身往沼泽方向，在泥浆中深一脚浅一脚地艰难移动。他也跟了过来，我们一前一后前往沼泽深处。不知过了多久，我听到了警车声，过了一会儿，一个声音向我们喊话：

“你们是孪生兄弟还是幽灵人？”

我俩都没有做声。

“按照第三十三条法令，一旦发现幽灵人，我们需要立刻射杀，包括和他们接触的所有人，防止更多人的记忆开始松动和生锈，你们到底是不是幽灵人？”

“不是” 我们两个异口同声。

我想让他活着，在剩下的生命里一遍遍悔恨。我感到他也笑了一下，没错，我们都一样恶毒。

In many nights, there are two of me in my dreams. At gatherings, when someone calls my name, I lift my head, only to see another me doing the same, responding in my stead, while I sit silently in the corner, and no one finds this odd.

Two of me, once I open my eyes, I can see the other, the kaleidoscope-like dream leaves me dizzy and exhausted. There is always a group of people partying in the dream, and I endure the torment of identity while sipping on bitter alcohol in a corner. In one dream, I meet another unfortunate soul in a corner, facing the same predicament as mine. I asked him if killing the doppelgänger works. He said he has tried; those you kill in private immediately grow back, for he has already taken over your essence. Only by killing him publicly, in front of everyone, can you truly ensure he never comes back to life. As he spoke, he looked at his own duplicate.

The room was filled with joy and laughter. In the dim corner, he gestured to me from under the table; he wanted to commence a massacre to erase these absurd memories from everyone’s dreams. I understood his intention. Soon, an opportunity turned off the lights. In the darkness, I heard the sounds of slaughter and blood.

When the lights came on, it was him who had died. It turned out that the replicas shared the same thoughts.

I quickly opened the window and jumped out, running away. I ran as fast as I could until I reached a marshland. Under the moonlight, I looked back to see another me, unable to make out his face, and I assumed neither could he.

I remembered all the wrongs I had committed. How many were subconscious impulses planted by him? I truly despised him.

“Don’t blame me just yet. Didn’t you enjoy yourself that time too?” He said in my mind.
“Shut up,” I snapped.
“By the way, that call that drove her mad, you didn’t know she was driving, did you?”
“You made me call her,” I squatted down, fiercely pounding a pit into the soft earth.

Ignoring him, I turned towards the marsh, struggling to move through the muck. He followed, and we headed deeper into the marsh. After what felt like an eternity, I heard sirens, and after a while, a voice shouted out to us:

“Are you twin brothers or phantom men?”

Neither of us made a sound.

“According to Article 33, once a phantom man is identified, we must shoot immediately, including anyone who has had contact with them, to prevent more people’s memories from becoming loose and rusty. Are you, or are you not, phantom men?”

“No,” we answered in unison.

I wanted him to stay alive, to feel remorse for the rest of his life. I sensed that he also chuckled a bit. Indeed, we were both equally malicious.
